Prompt — Temporarily Disable Follow‑Up Questions After FG / IDI (V1A Safety Lock)
Objective
Temporarily disable post‑execution follow‑up questions for FG and IDI studies in V1A to preserve a coherent research flow and avoid unscoped AI authority.
Follow‑up questions currently exist as an orphaned feature: they are not clearly anchored to framing, anchors, QA, or decision‑making, and they reopen generative AI interaction at a point where results should be interpreted—not modified.
This change is a V1A safety and coherence decision, not feature removal. The functionality may be re‑introduced later with explicit structure and intent.

Background / Rationale
At present:

After an FG or IDI finishes, users may ask up to 2 follow‑up questions
These questions:

do not alter study design
do not re‑open framing or anchors
are not constrained by templates or intent modes


This re‑introduces freeform AI authority at the most sensitive moment: after results are generated

Given the disciplined changes already made to Mark (intent‑bound help, phase‑aware presence, minimized chat), this unrestricted follow‑up step is inconsistent with the rest of the system.

Scope (IN)
1) Disable follow‑up question UI affordances
For FG and IDI studies:

Remove or disable:

follow‑up question input box
“Ask follow‑up question” buttons
remaining question counters (e.g. “2 questions remaining”)


This applies after:

synthetic FG execution
synthetic IDI execution



The goal is no generative input path post‑execution in V1A.

2) Preserve backend safety and reversibility

Do not delete follow‑up question code paths
Do not remove routes or data structures yet
Do not refactor execution logic

This is a feature flag / UI gating decision, not a destructive change.

3) User‑facing messaging (important)
Replace the follow‑up UI with a clear, brief explanation:

Follow‑up questions are temporarily unavailable in V1A.
This release focuses on getting the study framing right before execution.
Clarification and iteration features will return in a future update.

This avoids confusion and reinforces good research behavior.

Scope (OUT)

❌ Do not introduce a “1 question only” compromise
❌ Do not reroute follow‑ups into Mark chat
❌ Do not modify Mark prompts or reasoning
❌ Do not change telemetry schemas or events
❌ Do not alter FG / IDI execution behavior
❌ Do not add new forms of interpretation or explanation logic

The goal is removal of ambiguity, not substitution.

Definition of Done

✅ After FG or IDI completes:

No follow‑up question input is available
No question counter is shown


✅ Users are informed why the feature is disabled
✅ Results, summaries, personas, and outputs are unchanged
✅ No AI interaction is possible post‑execution in V1A
✅ The change can be reversed later without data loss


How to Test

Run a synthetic FG study
View results screen
Confirm:

no follow‑up question input exists
explanatory message is shown instead


Repeat for a synthetic IDI
Confirm nothing in execution, summaries, or personas is affected


Design Principle (for reference)

AI should not reopen the research process after execution unless the user is explicitly starting a new research action.
