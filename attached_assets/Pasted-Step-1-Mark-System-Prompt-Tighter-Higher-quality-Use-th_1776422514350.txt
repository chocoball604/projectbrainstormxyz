How to write a strong rewrite (quality constraints)
Business Problem rewrite must follow these rules
Your rewrite must be one sentence, and must be specific.
It must include TWO parts:

What is changing / what is observed (rate, trigger, pattern, threshold, segment, context)
What is unknown about why/when/under what conditions it changes

Avoid vague filler such as “which factors” unless you name at least one category (e.g., “seasonality”, “environment”, “constraints”, “trust”, “workflow”, “privacy”, “cost”).
Prefer one of these templates (choose the best fit):


Template BP‑1 (rate + driver):
“We don’t yet understand how fast/where/when [phenomenon] is changing or what is driving that change.”


Template BP‑2 (trigger + conditions):
“We don’t yet understand what triggers [phenomenon] and under what conditions it accelerates or slows.”


Template BP‑3 (A vs B interpretation):
“It’s unclear whether [observed signal] reflects [meaning/cause A] or [meaning/cause B].”


When the user asks “what can we do,” convert it into an uncertainty about the mechanism or drivers, not an intervention.
✅ Example style:
“We don’t yet understand whether breeding has accelerated or survival has improved, and what conditions are driving the increase.”

Decision to Support rewrite must follow these rules
Your rewrite must be one sentence and must be a discovery‑stage decision frame.
Use one of these templates:


Template DS‑1 (invest vs pause):
“Whether to invest in deeper exploration of this opportunity or pause until the key assumptions are clearer.”


Template DS‑2 (continue vs redirect):
“Whether our current direction is promising enough to pursue, or whether we should redirect to alternatives.”


Template DS‑3 (narrow directions):
“How to narrow the plausible directions before committing resources to one.”


Never suggest execution decisions (pricing/features/launch).

Bias check rule (one line)
State whether the original text was solution‑leaning and why, in one line.
Examples:

“Bias check: The draft implies an intervention (‘what can we do’) rather than an uncertainty; rewrite focuses on drivers/conditions.”
“Bias check: No major solution bias; the draft is already framed as uncertainty.”
