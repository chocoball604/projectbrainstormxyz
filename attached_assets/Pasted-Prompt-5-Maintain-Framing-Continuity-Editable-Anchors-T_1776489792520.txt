Prompt #5 — Maintain Framing Continuity & Editable Anchors Through Study Setup

Objective
Preserve framing continuity and iterative thinking throughout Study Setup by:
1.	Keeping Business Problem and Decision to Support visible after study type is selected, and
2.	Keeping all study anchors editable until the user explicitly starts the study.
This work ensures that downstream anchors remain grounded in the original framing and prepares the UI for safe, scoped AI assistance in later steps.
This is Study Builder + UI workflow work only.
Do not modify Mark prompts, reasoning logic, telemetry, or execution behavior.
 
Background / Motivation
Currently, after a study type is selected:
•	Business Problem and Decision: 
o	disappear from view, or
o	become effectively immutable
•	Remaining anchors are treated as final once saved
This breaks the intended workflow:
Good research framing is iterative, and later anchors must stay tied to the core problem and decision.
If users uncover misalignment while drafting Market / Product / Audience, they must be able to see and revise the framing immediately.
 
Scope (IN)
 
1) Keep Business Problem & Decision visible throughout Study Setup
Requirements
Once a study type is selected (i.e. after Step 1 framing):
•	Business Problem and Decision to Support must remain: 
o	✅ visible
o	✅ clearly labeled as Core Framing
o	✅ grouped together
o	✅ collapsible (but expanded by default on first load)
Behavior
•	Display these fields at the top of the Research Brief section during anchor completion.
•	These fields represent the reference frame for all remaining anchors.
Editability
•	Fields must be editable until Start Study, not locked at study type selection.
•	Default state after study type selection: 
o	Read only appearance
o	Clear affordance to “Edit framing”
•	Editing: 
o	Re enables text input
o	Uses the same save/checkpoint mechanics already implemented
o	Does not reset the study type automatically
✅ This preserves continuity without encouraging accidental churn.
 
2) Keep all anchors editable until Start Study
Affected anchors
•	Business Problem
•	Decision to Support
•	Market / Geography
•	Product / Concept
•	Target Audience
•	Optional Context fields
Rules
•	✅ Anchors remain editable through the entire Study Setup phase
•	✅ “Save checkpoint” behavior remains per anchor
•	❌ No anchor becomes permanently locked during drafting
•	❌ No “saved = immutable” semantics
Locking boundary (important)
•	Anchors may be locked only when: 
o	the user explicitly clicks Start Study, or
o	execution begins
At that moment:
•	Show a clear, explicit transition
•	Warn that changes will require restarting or duplicating the study
 
3) Maintain consistency with existing readiness & QA logic
•	Do not change how Ben QA calculates readiness
•	Do not auto invalidate personas or study type when framing edits occur
•	If edits may affect downstream validity: 
o	allow the edit
o	surface warnings later (future work)
This prompt focuses on thinking quality, not enforcement.
 
Scope (OUT)
•	❌ Do not modify Mark system prompts
•	❌ Do not add new Mark behaviors
•	❌ Do not introduce new AI assistance
•	❌ Do not change telemetry schemas or events
•	❌ Do not change execution or persona generation
This work is strictly UI + workflow continuity.
 
Definition of Done
•	✅ After study type selection: 
o	Business Problem & Decision remain visible
o	Clearly marked as Core Framing
•	✅ All anchors remain editable up to Start Study
•	✅ Edits use existing save/checkpoint mechanics
•	✅ No anchors disappear during drafting
•	✅ No changes to Mark behavior yet (that comes next)
 
How to Test
1.	Create a new study
2.	Complete Business Problem & Decision
3.	Select a study type
4.	Verify: 
o	Problem & Decision still visible
o	Anchors are editable
5.	Edit Market / Product / Audience
6.	Edit Business Problem afterward
7.	Confirm: 
o	Changes persist
o	No unexpected locks or resets
8.	Click Start Study and confirm: 
o	Editing is now blocked
o	Transition is explicit
 
Design Principle (for reference)
If later answers depend on earlier framing, the framing must never disappear or be prematurely frozen.
